Dear Emily, When I am without you it feels as if I am a spectator to my own life. The days blur past me as I reminisce and anticipate our time together. But when we are finally united, I cherish every moment and detail: the slight part in your hair, the gentle furrowing of your brows while you peruse the menu. How can someone be so effortlessly endearing? I snap a picture in a futile attempt to capture the scene, You catch me. We laugh. We end up at the park, sitting on a bench. You wrap around my arm, your head falls upon my shoulder. You tell me you love me, but I already know: by the way your skin brushes mine and the hydrangeas bloom. The sun beats down as to evict us. We leave, but I remain. Love, Frank
